Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years) and is typically more affordable. Whole life insurance provides permanent coverage for your entire life and includes a cash value component that grows over time. The best choice depends on your budget, goals, and family situation — I can help you determine which is right for you.

A common rule of thumb is 10-15 times your annual income, but the right amount depends on factors like your debts, number of dependents, future expenses (like college tuition), and your spouse's income. Yes! Many carriers now offer no-exam life insurance policies with quick approvals — sometimes in as little as 24 hours. These policies are ideal if you want coverage fast or have health concerns that make traditional underwriting challenging. Coverage amounts typically go up to $500K or more.

Life insurance is often more affordable than people think. A healthy 30-year-old can get a $500,000 20-year term policy for as little as $20-30/month. Your rate depends on age, health, coverage amount, and policy type. I shop multiple carriers to find you the best rate available.

The best time is now. Life insurance premiums are based on your age and health — the younger and healthier you are when you apply, the lower your rates will be. Waiting even a few years can significantly increase your costs. Lock in your rate today while it's at its lowest.

There are several types: HMO (Health Maintenance Organization), PPO (Preferred Provider Organization), EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization), and HDHP (High Deductible Health Plan) with HSA options. Each has different trade-offs between cost, flexibility, and provider networks. I'll help you understand which type best fits your healthcare needs and budget.

An annuity is a financial product issued by an insurance company that provides guaranteed income, typically for retirement. You contribute money (either as a lump sum or over time), and the insurer pays you back in regular installments, either immediately or at a future date. It's one of the only financial products that can guarantee you won't outlive your money.

The three main types are: Fixed annuities (guaranteed interest rate, predictable growth), Indexed annuities (returns linked to a market index like the S&P 500 with downside protection), and Variable annuities (invested in market subaccounts with higher risk/reward potential). I primarily work with fixed and indexed annuities for their balance of growth and safety.

Fixed and indexed annuities are among the safest retirement vehicles available. Your principal is protected by the issuing insurance company, and fixed annuities guarantee a minimum interest rate. Indexed annuities offer market-linked growth with a floor that protects against losses. I only recommend products from highly rated, financially strong insurance carriers.

Annuities can benefit people at various stages, but they're most commonly purchased between ages 45-70. Starting earlier allows more time for tax-deferred growth, while those closer to retirement can use annuities for immediate income. There's no one-size-fits-all answer — I'll help you determine if now is the right time based on your financial goals.

Annuity earnings grow tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes on gains until you withdraw the money. When you do take withdrawals, the earnings portion is taxed as ordinary income. If you purchased the annuity with after-tax dollars, your original contributions (cost basis) are not taxed again. This tax-deferred growth can significantly boost your retirement savings over time.
